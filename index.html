Tactical concepts

Attacking Tactics

False nine

A false nine is a striker who drops deep, disrupting defenders and creating space for teammates.

Tiki-Taka

Short, quick passes and movement, maintaining possession to break down defenses.

Counter-Attack

Quick transitions from defense to attack, exploiting opponents' advanced positions after regaining possession.

Overlapping Runs

Fullbacks and wingers create width and crossing chances by running past teammates on the flanks.

Inverted Wingers

Wingers play on the opposite flank of their stronger foot, cutting inside to shoot or create attacking opportunities.

Direct Play

Quickly advancing the ball upfield with long passes and fast movements, bypassing midfield.

Defensive Tactics

High Press

An aggressive tactic where the team applies pressure high up the pitch to win possession quickly.

Man Marking vs Zonal Marking

Man-marking tracks individual opponents, while zonal marking focuses on defending specific areas.

Offside Trap

Defenders skillfully step up as a unit to catch attackers offside and disrupt their dangerous runs.

Deep-Lying Playmaker

A midfielder positioned deep, dictating play with long passes and controlling tempo.

Sweeper Keeper

A goalkeeper who actively participates in defense, often coming off their line to clear or intercept.

Low Block

A compact defensive shape where players sit deep to absorb pressure and reduce space for opponents.
