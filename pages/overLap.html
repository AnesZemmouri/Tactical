Understanding Overlapping Runs

What are Overlapping Runs?

Overlapping runs involve a player, typically a full-back or wing-back, making a run beyond a teammate who is in possession of the ball. This movement aims to create additional space and provide more attacking options by stretching the opposition's defensive line.

History and Evolution

The concept of overlapping runs has been utilized in various tactical systems throughout football history. Teams like Barcelona and Borussia Dortmund have effectively used overlapping full-backs to enhance their attacking play and create numerical advantages on the flanks.

Why Use Overlapping Runs?

Overlapping runs can disrupt the opponent's defensive organization by forcing them to choose between marking the ball carrier or the overlapping player. This can lead to the creation of space and opportunities for crossing or cutting inside.

Tactical Considerations

Successful overlapping runs require good timing, communication, and understanding between the players involved. The overlapping player must make their run at the right moment to avoid being caught offside and to maximize their impact on the attack.
